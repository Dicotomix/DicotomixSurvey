This is a presentation from Association du Locked-In Syndrome on the various ways for a locked-in person to communicate. It mentions writing, pointing, and communicating with only "yes" and "no", using various alphabets, with methodology advice.
It also presents telethesis, which are devices helping them communicate, but which often assume more mobility than a simple "yes" or "no". This is also the case for the improvements presented on computers.
We are also given a few softwares for spelling on computers and tablet.
At the end, there a few comments from non-speaking people about their experience, which can be interesting to read before meeting a locked-in person.
